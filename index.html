FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions? I have compiled answers to the most common inquiries I receive from authors.

How quickly will I see results from your services?

Results timelines vary depending on your starting point and the service you choose. Most clients begin seeing noticeable improvements within 2-4 weeks including increased visibility, more reviews, and climbing rankings. However, sustainable, long-term success is typically built over 60-90 days. I will set realistic expectations during our initial assessment.

Do you guarantee bestseller status or specific sales numbers?

I am committed to honesty and transparency, which means I do not make guarantees about specific rankings or sales numbers. There are too many variables outside anyone's control. What I do guarantee is strategic, data-driven marketing that significantly improves your book visibility and positions it for maximum success.

What genres do you work with?

I work with authors across virtually all genres including fiction (romance, thriller, mystery, science fiction, fantasy, literary fiction, and more) and non-fiction (business, self-help, memoir, health, how-to, and others). Each genre has its own marketing quirks and optimal strategies, and I have developed expertise across the spectrum.

I have already launched my book and it flopped. Is it too late?

Absolutely not! Many of my most successful client transformations have been rescue projects for books that launched poorly and seemed destined for obscurity. A failed launch is not a death sentence for your book. It is an opportunity for a strategic relaunch. I have helped countless authors turn around failed books into consistent sellers and even bestsellers.

Are your review generation methods compliant with Amazon terms?

100% yes. All my review generation strategies are completely compliant with Amazon terms of service and industry best practices. I never use review exchanges, paid reviews, or any other tactics that could put your account at risk. My methods focus on legitimate strategies: building genuine Advanced Reader Teams, leveraging ethical book review services, and creating systems that encourage organic reviews.

How involved do I need to be in the process?

This depends on the service you choose and your preferences. Some of my services are highly hands-off where I do the work and you focus on writing. Others require more collaboration, especially during the strategy and planning phases. At minimum, you will need to be available for initial consultations, provide necessary materials, and review or approve strategies before implementation.
